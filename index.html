4Paws for Ability

The Sweetest Service

4 Paws for Ability enriches the lives of children with disabilities by training and placing quality, task-trained service dogs. This provides increased independence for the children, and assistance to their families. And 4 Paws also works with veterans from recent conflicts who’ve lost the use of their limbs or their hearing while in active combat.

Learn More

History

When a rare neurological disorder left her confined to a wheelchair, Karen Shirk sought out a service dog. She applied to 3 different agencies and was rejected each time for being “too disabled.” With the help of a professional trainer, Shirk trained her own service dog.

Later, Shirk formed 4Paws for Ability, which differentiates itself by having no age or disability restrictions. Since founding the organization in 1998, 4 Paws has placed over 650 dogs in seven countries.

Capabilities

The dogs at 4Paws are trained for a variety of services. Most people think service dogs are seeing-eye exclusive, but 4Paws dogs specialize in many other areas. These range from hearing aid dogs, autism and mobility assistance, seizure and diabetic alert dogs, and dogs for veterans.

4Paws also allows international adoptions, and 95% of its recipients are children. These are just a few of the ways 4Paws stands apart from other service dog organizations.

Successes

Today, 4Paws has a 90% success rate in the placement of service dogs to alert for seizures minutes to several hours before a seizure happens. Though they focus mainly on children, their veteran program has given many veterans their independence back.

Today, 4Paws places approximately 100 dogs a year, and has retained its primary mission of having no age requirements and never turning down a child who does not “fit” into a traditional category.
